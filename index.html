About the EIAH Team

The EIAH Research Team (Environnement Informatique pour l’Apprentissage Humain – Computer-Based Learning Environments for Human Learning) is a research team within the LMCS Laboratory at the École nationale Supérieure d’Informatique (ESI), Algiers.

The team conducts research structured around three main axes:

  • Design of Educational Resources: Supporting teachers in instructional design and pedagogical content creation using authoring tools, development of language courses, serious games, and research on assessment methodologies.
  • Tutoring and Learner Monitoring: Personalization and adaptation of learning environments, learning trace analysis, learner annotations, and intelligent tutoring systems.
  • Tools and Models for EIAH: Learner modeling, pedagogical ontologies, semantic representation of educational knowledge, and integration of web services in EIAH systems.

The EIAH team is multidisciplinary, bringing together researchers in computer science, electronics, mathematics, and English. Research topics reflect both current scientific challenges in EIAH and the specific needs of ESI.

LMCS Laboratory

The LMCS Laboratory (Laboratory of Methods for Systems Design) was established in 1999 and is affiliated with the École nationale Supérieure d’Informatique (ESI), Algiers.

LMCS research activities are organized around:

  • Methods: Optimization, Knowledge Representation, Classification, Robustness, and related formal and computational approaches.
  • Systems Design: Information Systems, Embedded Systems, Hypermedia Systems, Communicating Systems, and Recognition Systems.

The EIAH team contributes to LMCS research through the design and development of intelligent and adaptive learning systems.

Research Areas

Educational Content Engineering

Instructional design support, authoring tools, serious games, language learning systems, and digital assessment technologies.

Adaptive & Intelligent Learning Systems

Personalization, learner tracking, trace analysis, intelligent tutoring systems, and adaptive EIAH platforms.

Modeling & Semantic Technologies

Learner modeling, pedagogical ontologies, knowledge representation, and web-based services for educational environments.
