About the TEL Team

The TEL Research Team (Technology Enhanced Learning) is a research team within the LMCS Laboratory
√âcole nationale Sup√©rieure d‚ÄôInformatique (ESI), Algiers

The team conducts research in the field of computer-based learning environments ‚Äî structured around three main research axes:

  • Educational Content Design: Assisting teachers in instructional design and pedagogical content creation using authoring tools, developing language courses, serious games, and addressing digital assessment challenges.
  • Tutoring & Learner Monitoring: Personalization and adaptation of learning environments, learner trace analysis, annotations, and intelligent tutoring systems.
  • Models & Tools for Learning: Learner modeling, pedagogical ontologies, semantic knowledge representation, and web services integration within learning environments.

The TEL team is multidisciplinary, including researchers in computer science, electronics, mathematics, and English. Research themes reflect both current scientific challenges in Technology Enhanced Learning and the specific needs of ESI.

LMCS Laboratory

The LMCS Laboratory (Laboratory of Methods for Systems Design), established in 1999, is affiliated with the ESI Algiers.

  • Methods: Optimization, Knowledge Representation, Classification, Robustness, and formal computational approaches.
  • Systems Design: Information Systems, Embedded Systems, Hypermedia Systems, Communicating Systems, and Recognition Systems.

Research Areas

Instructional Engineering

Authoring tools, serious games, language learning systems, and digital assessment technologies.

Adaptive Learning Systems

Personalization, learner analytics, trace analysis, and intelligent tutoring systems.

Knowledge & Learner Modeling

Ontologies, semantic technologies, learner models, and web-based educational services.

Artificial Intelligence (IA)

Machine learning, predictive modeling, and intelligent decision support for personalised learning pathways and data analysis.

Visualization & Visual Analytics

Interactive dashboards, educational data visualization, visual mining of learner traces, and explainable AI interfaces.

Healthcare Analytics

Computational methods for clinical data analysis, patient monitoring, and decision support in medical learning environments.
